The Addis Ababa Institute of Technology (AAiT), a prominent branch of Addis Ababa University, has been a cornerstone of engineering education in Ethiopia since its establishment in 1953. Originally known as the Imperial College of Engineering, it began by offering two-year intermediate engineering programs, subsequently expanding to four-year degree programs in civil and industrial engineering in 1958. Over the years, AAiT has evolved to encompass a wide array of engineering disciplines, reflecting its commitment to advancing technological education in the nation. ([aait.edu.et](https://aait.edu.et/about-aait?utm_source=chatgpt.com))

**Vision and Mission**

AAiT aspires to be among the top five technology higher education institutions in Africa. Its mission encompasses educating students to become leading engineers and entrepreneurs, pursuing research that fosters innovation, and contributing to the economic and social transformation of Ethiopia and beyond. The institute is dedicated to incubating technology transfers that enhance productivity and quality in local industries, fostering a culture of achievement, and serving the community by advancing scientific and technological literacy. ([aait.edu.et](https://aait.edu.et/about-aait?utm_source=chatgpt.com))

**Academic Structure**

The institute is organized into five schools and several specialized centers:

- **Schools:**
  - School of Civil and Environmental Engineering
  - School of Electrical and Computer Engineering
  - School of Mechanical and Industrial Engineering
  - School of Chemical and Bio Engineering
  - School of Multidisciplinary Engineering

- **Centers:**
  - Center of Biomedical Engineering
  - Center of Energy Technology
  - Center of Railway Engineering
  - Center of Materials Engineering

These schools and centers offer a range of undergraduate and postgraduate programs designed to meet the evolving needs of the engineering sector. ([aait.edu.et](https://aait.edu.et/about-aait?utm_source=chatgpt.com))

**Academic Programs**

AAiT provides a comprehensive array of programs across various engineering disciplines. At the undergraduate level, students can pursue Bachelor of Science degrees in fields such as Civil Engineering, Electrical and Computer Engineering, Mechanical Engineering, Chemical Engineering, and Biomedical Engineering. For postgraduate studies, the institute offers Master of Science (MSc) and Doctor of Philosophy (PhD) programs in specialized areas including Structural Engineering, Geotechnical Engineering, Hydraulic Engineering, and more. ([portal.aait.edu.et](https://portal.aait.edu.et/Web/ProgramList?utm_source=chatgpt.com))

**Admission Requirements**

Admission to AAiT's undergraduate programs is processed through the Ministry of Education of Ethiopia. Students who have completed the 10+2 years preparatory program and have met the minimum cut-off point set by the Ministry are eligible to join the Pre-engineering program at AAiT. After completing the first semester of this program, students can choose their specific engineering discipline based on their performance and preferences. The institute also offers admission opportunities for diploma graduates from Technical and Vocational Education and Training (TVET) institutions and international students, subject to specific criteria. **Research and Collaboration**

AAiT is actively involved in research and has established partnerships with various international institutions and organizations. For instance, the Center of Biomedical Engineering collaborates with universities such as the University of Oulu and Tampere University of Technology in Finland, as well as the University of Cape Town in South Africa. These collaborations focus on capacity development projects and aim to advance research in areas like biomedical instrumentation, rehabilitation, and computing.
